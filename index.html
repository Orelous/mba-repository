🧠 The Three T's — match the mode to the day

Same repository, four different ways to query it. Each mode is a different cognitive task. Using Test every day is what plateaus your learning by Week 6 — the sessions must change character across the week.

📐 How the four pillars actually get hit each week

📏
Spacing
Same-day (Mon PM, 12hr after study) + 1-day (Tue) + multi-day (pre-class + post-class) creates the actual spacing intervals. Tier 1/2/3 in the app respects this once entries are rated.
🧪
Retrieval Practice
Every session after Monday Study is production-before-reveal — never passive review. Teach mode is the highest difficulty: full explanation from a scenario with no cue.
🔀
Interleaving
Tier filters shuffle across courses automatically. Post-class day adds a dedicated Interleaved Session (cross-course bridging with AI synthesis) — this is where the four-pillar integration actually happens.
🧭
Decision Framework
Built into every entry: USE WHEN, AVOID WHEN, failure patterns, client scenarios, cross-course connections. Teach mode forces all five to come out of your mouth, not just be recognized on screen.

⚠️ What breaks the system

  • Skipping Monday Study Block. Testing on Monday afternoon before encoding means you blank on every new entry. Encoding before retrieval, always. If Monday morning is impossible, move Study to Sunday evening after import — but do not skip it.
  • Skipping the Monday PM Same-Day session. The 12-hour interval after morning study is the first spacing gap. Skip it and Tuesday is already catching up to forgetting instead of reinforcing fresh encoding.
  • Treating Tier Practice as the only practice. Interleaved Session (post-class day) is where cross-course bridging happens — that's the Interleaving pillar. Without it you have tier-spaced retrieval but no integration. Warm-Up Retrieval (3 min before any session) primes recall and costs almost nothing.
  • Using Test mode every day instead of rotating Test → Target → Teach. Test on Friday instead of Teach means you never push to production fluency. The sessions must change character across the week.
  • Inflating ratings. A 4 in the app that you can't deliver in front of an HVAC contractor is a 2. Rate what comes out of your mouth, not what you recognize. Target mode depends on honest ratings to find real weak points.
  • Ignoring Tier 2 in favor of Tier 1. Tier 1 feels urgent because it's new. Tier 2 (post-class day) is the actual reinforcement sweet spot — the spacing interval where consolidation happens. Don't let Tier 1 starve Tier 2.
  • Skipping the Sunday export. Browser localStorage can wipe with no warning. Export JSON monthly minimum, weekly preferred. Treat it like ANOH client IP.
💡
Marginal Gains Philosophy: Consistency beats intensity. Track your process (did you show up?), not outcomes (was it perfect?). 3-minute sessions count fully.
